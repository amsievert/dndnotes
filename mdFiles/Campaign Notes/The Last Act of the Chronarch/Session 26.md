Stone 1: Herein we document the story of our people, we dwarves of the earth, scorned by our Goddess. This land is unforgiving, but we are a stubborn people who refuse to fade into the dark, erased from the annals of time, that cruel scribe. It is said that the race of dwarves were the first of Genesis's creations to stand up to the destruction of Loathsome One-Eye, great destroyer. This is our people, solid and unbreaking as the rock we live within, and here I preserve our legacy. I am the scrivener Hjald Faldir and these are my people, the forsaken denizens of Mor Torum.

Stone 2: For as long as the Elders of our Elders can remember, we have been a people endowed by the Mistress of Fortune, our glorious divine Lady Reicha. She instilled in us the love for all that shines and provided us with the means to satiate it. In return we built a great city, filled with grand cathedrals and adorned with more precious metals than even the palaces of the Gods. This was the great city of Mor Torum.
